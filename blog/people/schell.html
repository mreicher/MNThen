Schell, August (1828 – 1891)

Creator: Matt Reicher
Published: December 14, 2025 Updated: December 14, 2025

August Schell, a German immigrant and member of the Turner Colonization Society, arrived in newly established New Ulm as part of a deliberate effort to build a frontier city rooted in German values. While there, he founded one of the state's earliest commercial breweries. Despite navigating numerous pioneer-era challenges, Schell prospered, his self-made success marked by generosity, thoughtfulness, and a lifelong love of the outdoors.

Schell was born on February 15, 1828, in Durbach, Baden, Germany, to Carolus "Karl" Schell and Maria Anna Vicellio. His father was the head forester near Durbach in the Black Forest, and Schell spent his childhood surrounded by trees and animals. In the years after his father's 1837 death, he learned the machinist trade.

A twenty-year-old Schell immigrated to the United States in 1848, initially settling in New Orleans, Louisiana. He struggled to find work in the region and eventually traveled to Cincinnati, Ohio, to join a growing German-American settlement. Schell became a machinist for the Cincinnati Locomotive Works. On March 7, 1853, he married Theresia (Theresa) Hermann. The couple's first two children, daughters Emilie and Emma, were born in the area in 1854 and 1856.

In 1856 the family of four moved to New Ulm. As part of the Turner Colonization Society of Cincinnati, Schell joined others working to establish an American frontier city built on staunch German values. He was involved with the milling industry during his first four years in New Ulm. Schell worked as a machinist and supervisor for the Cincinnati Globe Mill Company.

Eldest son Adolph was born in New Ulm on February 12, 1858. Two years later, on July 19, 1860, daughter Anna was born.

The mill had significant financial issues within a year of its inception. The German Land Company, its ownership group, searched for a buyer to take over the business. Schell, believing the problems were because of material and equipment debts instead of the business itself, leased the company. Although he achieved some success, he soon realized brewing beer for the predominantly German community would be a more profitable venture.

In 1860, Schell purchased land in a heavily wooded area with an artesian spring near the banks of the Cottonwood River to start his brewery. Construction began in January 1861, and by that fall the brewery was open for business. It was financed through barter, trading lumber and services for land. In the company’s first year of business, Schell and his business partner Jacob Bernhardt, former brewmaster of Saint Paul's Benzberg Brewery, produced two hundred barrels of beer.

The land Schell built the brewery on was in the heart of Dakota territory. In 1862, it became part of the Dakota Conflict. After the second attack on New Ulm in August, residents were evacuated to Mankato, Saint Peter, and Saint Paul. Upon their return, they found their homes and businesses had been destroyed. What wasn't burned down was ransacked. The brewery, located beyond the town’s limits, was spared, likely due to Mrs. Schell’s established goodwill with the Dakota.

On July 15, 1862, the Schell family welcomed their second son, Otto. Their youngest child, a daughter named Augusta, was born two years later.

In 1866, Bernhardt fell ill and decided to sell his ownership stake in the brewery. To ensure the greatest financial return for his partner, Schell offered his half for sale as well. At a public auction held on August 3, 1866, August Schell submitted the winning bid of $12,000, becoming the sole proprietor of the brewery.

The company flourished over the next several years, but the Schell family was not without tragedy. Nine-year-old Anna passed away on March 31, 1870, and seven years later, at the age of fifty, Schell developed severe rheumatoid arthritis. He struggled to move and could no longer perform his duties as the company owner. Schell turned over many of the brewery's day-to-day business responsibilities to sons Adolph and Otto. He remained its chief executive.

Schell’s Brewery continued to grow despite increasing local competition. By the 1870s, the brewery grounds included seven stone cellars and four ice houses, capable of storing 700 tons of river ice and enabling year-round lagering. Otto Schell, who had studied brewing in Germany, directed the brewery’s rapid expansion.

For years the Schell family lived in a small home on the brewery grounds. In 1885, August built a ten-room mansion with a deer park nearby, recreating the Black Forest of his childhood. The new house cost $5000.

On September 20, 1891, Schell died of heart failure. His wife Theresa became the owner, and their son Otto managed the brewery. When Otto incorporated the business in October 1902, he formalized family control. The brewery survived Prohibition and remains family-owned today.

Quick Facts

  • Born: February 15, 1828, Durbach, Baden, Germany
  • Died: September 20, 1891, New Ulm, Minnesota (Age 63)
  • Known For: Founder of the August Schell Brewing Company; Turner Society leader; civic figure in early New Ulm
  • Key Moments:
    • Immigrated to U.S. in 1848
    • Helped found New Ulm in 1856
    • Opened brewery in 1861 with Jacob Bernhardt
    • Sole owner by 1866
    • Built Schell Mansion (1885)
    • Brewery survived Dakota Conflict and Prohibition
  • Spouse: Theresa Hermann (m. 1853–1891)
  • Children: Emilie, Emma, Adolph, Anna (d. 1870), Otto, Augusta
  • Legacy: Second-oldest family-owned brewery in the U.S.; enduring symbol of German-Minnesotan resilience and enterprise

Timeline

  • Feb 15, 1828: Born in Durbach, Germany
  • 1848: Emigrated to U.S., settled in Cincinnati
  • Mar 7, 1853: Married Theresa Hermann

  • 1860: Purchased land for brewery; partnered with Jacob Bernhardt
  • 1861: Brewery opened; produced 200 barrels in first year
  • Aug 1862: Dakota Conflict spares brewery due to Schell family goodwill
  • 1866: Acquired full ownership at public auction for $12,000
  • 1877: Developed severe arthritis; sons assume operational roles
  • 1885: Built Schell Mansion and deer park
  • Sep 20, 1891: Died in New Ulm; Theresa and Otto continue leadership
  • 1902: Brewery incorporated under family control
