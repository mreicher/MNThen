Olson, Floyd B. (1891 – 1936)

Creator: Matt Reicher
Published: July 17, 2023 Updated: July 17, 2023

Floyd B. Olson—Minnesota’s only Farmer-Labor Governor—stood as a beacon of hope during the darkest days of the Great Depression. A fierce advocate for the common worker, he transformed state government into a tool for economic justice and social reform. Though his tenure was tragically brief, his legacy as a champion of the people endures, honored by a memorial at the Minnesota State Capitol.

Born on November 13, 1891, in Minneapolis, Floyd Bjørnstjerne Olson grew up amid the struggles of a working-class family. This early exposure to hardship shaped his lifelong commitment to social justice and the rights of laborers. He attended the University of Minnesota, where he honed his oratory skills and developed a deep understanding of the challenges facing working Minnesotans.

Olson’s political career began in earnest in 1919 when he was appointed as a special assistant to the Hennepin County Attorney. He was elected Hennepin County Attorney the following year, where he gained a reputation for his unwavering pursuit of justice and his dedication to protecting workers' rights. His progressive stance and effectiveness in office positioned him as a rising star in Minnesota’s Farmer-Labor movement.

In 1930, Olson achieved a historic milestone by becoming the first member of the Farmer-Labor Party to be elected Governor of Minnesota. His administration was marked by a series of bold and transformative policies. He championed labor rights, actively supporting the right of workers to organize and bargain collectively. Under his leadership, the Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry was established to safeguard workers, enforce labor laws, and ensure safe working conditions.

Olson’s commitment to economic reform was crucial during the Great Depression. He supported public works programs to create jobs and alleviate suffering. His administration created the Minnesota Emergency Relief Administration, providing critical employment and relief to those devastated by the economic collapse. He also signed the Unemployment Insurance Act, offering a safety net for workers who lost their jobs.

Beyond economic policy, Olson was a vocal advocate for civil liberties and social justice. He fought against discrimination and worked to secure equal rights for marginalized communities. His administration made groundbreaking appointments of African Americans to prominent state positions, reflecting his commitment to racial equality.

Tragically, Floyd B. Olson’s life and promising political career were cut short when he was diagnosed with stomach cancer in 1936. He passed away on August 22, 1936, at the age of 44, while still in office. His death was deeply mourned by the people of Minnesota, who recognized his transformative leadership.

In 1958, a memorial was dedicated at the Minnesota State Capitol to honor Governor Olson’s contributions. The Floyd B. Olson Memorial features a bronze statue crafted by Italian-American sculptors Carlo Brioschi and his son Amerigo. Located on the Capitol grounds, the memorial stands as a testament to Olson’s progressive legacy and his enduring impact on the state. It symbolizes the ongoing importance of fighting for economic justice, workers' rights, and equal opportunity.

Floyd B. Olson’s contributions to Minnesota’s history remain profound. His unwavering dedication to social justice, workers' rights, and economic reform left an indelible mark on the state’s political landscape. The memorial at the State Capitol ensures his memory lives on as a beacon of hope and inspiration for future generations committed to creating a more equitable society.

Quick Facts

  • Born: November 13, 1891, Minneapolis, Minnesota
  • Died: August 22, 1936, Saint Paul, Minnesota (Age 44)
  • Known For: 22nd Governor of Minnesota (1931–1936); First Farmer-Labor Party Governor
  • Key Achievements: • Established Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry
    • Created Emergency Relief Administration
    • Signed Unemployment Insurance Act
    • Pioneered progressive labor and social policies
  • Memorial: Floyd B. Olson Memorial at Minnesota State Capitol (1958)
  • Sculptors: Carlo Brioschi & Amerigo Brioschi
  • Legacy: Champion of workers' rights and economic justice during the Great Depression

Timeline

  • Nov 13, 1891: Born in Minneapolis, Minnesota

  • 1930: Elected 22nd Governor of Minnesota
  • 1931–1936: Served as Governor of Minnesota

  • Aug 22, 1936: Died in office at age 44
