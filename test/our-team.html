Matt Reicher

Founder & Historical Researcher

Growing up on the east side of St. Paul, Matt always felt a deep connection to Minnesota's history. What started as a childhood curiosity about the past only grew over the years, becoming a passion that shaped his life's work. After earning a BA in History from Metropolitan State University and an MA in Museum Studies from the University of Oklahoma, Matt knew exactly what he wanted to do: share the fascinating stories of Minnesota with anyone who'd listen.

To Matt, history isn't just about dates or famous figures. It's about the people who lived and worked here—the ones who might not have made it into the history books but still left a lasting impact on their communities. The small business owners who built legacies, the artists whose work lives on long after they're gone, the everyday people who shaped the world around them in ways that often go unnoticed.

When he's not pouring over old photographs or piecing together stories from old newspapers, you can probably find Matt canoeing down the St. Croix River or enjoying a cold Minnesota craft beer, chatting about local history with anyone who'll join in. Through Minnesota Then, Matt is doing what he loves most—helping fellow Minnesotans connect with our past in a way that feels personal, real, and alive.

Favorite Historical Collection?
The Minnesota Historical Society's oral history archives - they capture voices that might otherwise be lost to time.
Digital Preservation Challenge?
Balancing accessibility with preservation - making materials available while ensuring they last for generations.
Most Rewarding Project?
Digitizing a collection of letters from Minnesota soldiers during World War II that had been stored in an attic for decades.

Sarah Johnson

Digital Archivist

Sarah joined Minnesota Then with over a decade of experience in library science and digital archiving. With an MLIS from the University of Minnesota and a background in digital preservation, she's passionate about making historical materials accessible to everyone.

As our Digital Archivist, Sarah oversees the digitization of historical documents, photographs, and recordings. She works closely with local libraries, historical societies, and community members to preserve and share Minnesota's rich documentary heritage.

Sarah has pioneered several innovative digital preservation projects, including our oral history initiative that captures the memories of long-time Minnesota residents. Her work ensures that these valuable first-person accounts of Minnesota history will be available for generations to come.

A native of Duluth, Sarah brings a perspective from northern Minnesota to our team. When she's not digitizing historical materials, she enjoys hiking along the North Shore and researching her own family's history in the Iron Range.

Favorite Tech for History?
GIS mapping - it lets us layer historical information onto modern maps, creating a window through time.
Biggest Development Challenge?
Creating a mobile experience that works seamlessly across different devices while maintaining historical accuracy.
Future Tech Dreams?
Implementing augmented reality features that would let users see historical buildings and scenes overlaid on modern locations.

Michael Torres

Web Developer

Michael is the technical wizard behind Minnesota Then's digital presence. With a background in both computer science and history, he brings a unique perspective to our team that bridges technology and historical storytelling.

After graduating from the University of Minnesota with degrees in Computer Science and History, Michael worked for several tech startups before finding his perfect fit at Minnesota Then. Here, he can combine his technical expertise with his passion for local history.

As our Web Developer, Michael built and maintains our Museum Without Walls platform, ensuring that users have a seamless experience as they explore historical locations throughout Minnesota. He's constantly innovating and finding new ways to use technology to bring history to life.

Michael grew up in Rochester and maintains a special interest in the medical history of Minnesota. In his free time, he contributes to open-source projects and volunteers teaching coding skills to underserved youth in the Twin Cities.

Contribution Guidelines

What We're Looking For

Minnesota Then welcomes contributions that focus on local Minnesota history, with an emphasis on:

  • Untold or lesser-known stories about Minnesota communities
  • Historical events, places, or people with local significance
  • Personal connections to Minnesota's past
  • Cultural heritage and traditions of Minnesota's diverse communities
  • Historical context for current Minnesota landmarks or neighborhoods

Article Requirements

  • Length: 800-2,000 words
  • Well-researched with proper citations and sources
  • Original content not published elsewhere
  • Engaging, accessible writing style for general audiences
  • Include at least 2-3 relevant historical images (with proper permissions)

Submission Process

  1. Contact us with your article idea or pitch
  2. If approved, submit your draft article for review
  3. Our team will provide feedback and editing suggestions
  4. Once finalized, your article will be published on our blog with your byline
